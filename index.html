Honours thesis | University of Victoria
Uptown as a Transit Oriented Development? A Mixed-Methods Approach to Evaluating Transportation Practices and their Geographical Effects.

Transit-Oriented Development (TOD) is defined as a development that is located in close proximity to a public transit network, facilitates cycling and walking, and deters auto-use. Using Uptown Center in Victoria, BC as a case study, the objective of my Honour’s research was to identify the on-site transportation characteristics and contextual factors that may influence the transportation choices of site-users.
Paper: 76 pages
Plan 701 | University of Waterloo
The Evolution of Consultation Theory and Public Participation Practice: 1960-2013

The theory and practice surrounding public participation in planning has undergone considerable evolution since the 1960s. In this paper I discussed the evolution of public participation practice in planning and consultation theory in the contexts of broader social, political, and economic trends. While both public participation practice and consultation theory have evolved since the 1960s, I argued that the overall trajectories have differed due to the fundamental contrasts between the nature of theory and practice.
Paper: 16 pages
Masters Thesis | University of Waterloo
Smart Grid Deployment and Climate Change Response: Evaluating Climate Change Integration in Ontario’s Smart Grid Deployment Regime.

The purpose of my Master’s research was to use sector-wide content analysis to evaluate evidence of climate change integration within Ontario’s smart grid deployment regime between 2004 and 2013. Smart grid refers to modern electricity infrastructure with the capacity to facilitate mitigation and adaptation to climate change through technological, institutional and behavioral interventions. My objective was to identify areas of smart grid deployment where climate change integration could be strengthened and result in a positive and comprehensive response to climate change.
Paper: 290 pages
